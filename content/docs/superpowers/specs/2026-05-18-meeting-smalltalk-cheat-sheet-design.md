# Meeting Smalltalk Cheat Sheet Design

## Goal
Create a high-signal, quick-scan "Cheat Sheet" for meeting smalltalk and bonding, based on the `Smalltalk and bonding.md` guide. The output should be optimized for a Google Doc, allowing the user to find situational prompts instantly during a live call.

## Structure (Situational Grid)
The cheat sheet will be divided into the following sections:

### 1. The Opening (Warm-up)
- Focus: Generic but human catch-up phrases.
- Prompts for "Start of week" vs "Turbulent times".

### 2. Situational Modules (The "Look and Listen" section)
- **Background/Office**: Compliments for stylish or minimalist setups.
- **Pets**: Phrases for when a dog appears or barks.
- **Family/Kids**: Empathetic responses for family noises or appearances.

### 3. The Pivot (Professional Transition)
- Focus: Respecting time and leading into the agenda.
- Scripted transitions to move from chat to work.

### 4. Pro-Tips
- Quick reminders on "Matching Energy" and "50/50 Sharing".

## Format
- Markdown file (`.md`) formatted with clean headings and bullet points.
- Optimized for copy-pasting into Google Docs (uses standard MD headers).
